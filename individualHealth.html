Individual Health Insurance

Individual Health Insurance, also known as medical insurance, is a vital type of general insurance designed to shield you from financial setbacks caused by health conditions or medical emergencies, such as illnesses, diseases, or accidents.


This comprehensive coverage encompasses expenses related to pre and post-hospitalization, annual health check-ups, psychiatric support, critical illnesses, and maternity-related costs, tailored to suit your specific health insurance plan.


Think of it as having that reliable friend who's always there for you when you're unwell or feeling down. Your health is paramount, and prioritizing it ensures not just a better quality of life but also financial security. With the rise in pollution and population, diseases have become more prevalent. Moreover, in the hustle and bustle of daily life, it's easy to neglect health, often prioritizing work over well-being. However, neglecting health can lead to significant mental and financial strain, as untreated illnesses can escalate.


Medical expenses have soared due to inflation, making even basic medications costly. Additionally, the expenses associated with medical procedures have skyrocketed. Therefore, it's prudent to invest in Individual Health Insurance Plans.


Individual Health Insurance provides a safety net against the financial burden resulting from illnesses or injuries. These plans cover medication costs, pre and post-hospitalization expenses, surgical procedures, ambulance charges, and more incurred during the policy period. This coverage is crucial as quality treatment may be unaffordable otherwise.


With Individual Health Insurance, one can opt for reimbursement, wherein the insured pays initially and later submits the documents for reimbursement. Moreover, cashless facilities are available at network hospitals, covering diagnostic tests and ambulance charges up to a certain limit. Some plans even offer daycare facilities and coverage for pre-existing conditions, albeit with a waiting period.


The benefits of Individual Health Insurance are manifold:

  • Bonus of 5% for every claim-free year.
  • Waiver of medical tests for individuals under 45 years of age (subject to policy clauses).
  • Tax savings under Section 80D of the Income Tax Act.
  • Prompt reimbursement processing.
  • Comprehensive coverage for various medical expenses, including maternity-related costs.

Eligibility Criteria for Individual Health Insurance

The eligibility criteria for Individual Health Insurance Plans are outlined below:

â€¢ Entry Age:

Individuals aged between 18 to 65 years are eligible for Individual Health Insurance, though some policies may extend coverage up to 70 or beyond. Dependents, including children, can be covered from 90 days to 18 years of age. Premiums are determined based on age, with costs increasing as age advances.

â€¢ No Claim Bonus (NCB):

Individual Health Insurance policies offer a No Claim Bonus (NCB) to policyholders who do not make any claims during a policy year. This bonus typically amounts to 5% of the sum insured for each claim-free year. However, if a claim is made, the NCB benefit is forfeited. Joint coverage with a family member can qualify for this bonus under Individual Health Insurance Plans.

Medical expenses incurred during in-patient hospitalization are covered by Individual Health Insurance Plans.

â€¢ Pre and Post-Hospitalization Cost:

Individual Health Insurance covers medical expenses incurred both before and after hospitalization. This includes diagnostic tests (MRI, X-ray, etc.) and medications.

Exclusions of Individual Health Insurance Plans:

Some exclusions apply to Individual Health Insurance Plans:

  • Pre-existing conditions are covered only after a specified waiting period, which varies depending on the insurer and plan.
  • Coverage amounts for specific conditions are determined based on the severity and may range from 10 to 90 percent of the sum assured.li>
  • Non-allopathic treatments like Homeopathic and Ayurvedic therapies are typically not covered.
  • Conditions resulting from unhealthy habits such as smoking or alcohol consumption may not be covered.
  • Cosmetic procedures, except those resulting from accidents, are generally excluded.

Claim Process

When considering the purchase of an Individual Health Insurance Plan, understanding the claim process is essential. Family members may need to initiate the claim process if the insured individual is unwell. Typically, there are two methods followed for the claim process of Individual Health Insurance Plans:

â€¢ Cashless Claim Process:

In the cashless claim facility for Individual Health Insurance Plans, the insured individual must receive treatment in a network hospital specified during the purchase of the health plan. They need to present their e-card, health insurance plan details, and any relevant physical documents. Upon approval, the insured can avail themselves of cashless claim treatment. Approval is granted only if the injury or illness is covered under the Individual health plan. After discharge, the hospital submits all medical bills to the health insurance company for evaluation and payment.

â€¢ Reimbursement Claim Facility:

The second type of claim process in Individual Health Insurance Plans is the reimbursement facility. As the name suggests, the health insurance company provides reimbursement up to the sum assured for the amount already paid by the individual for treatment not covered under network hospitals. Here, the insured bears the initial treatment cost and submits all medical bills to the health insurance company for claims settlement. If the treatment is not covered, the claim may be rejected.

-- Common Health Insurance Terminologies Simplified:

  • Waiting Period: The duration you must wait before utilizing any benefits of your health insurance policy.
  • Copayment: Sharing of medical bills between you and your insurer, where you pay a portion while the insurer covers the rest.
  • Pre-Existing Disease: Any ailment you have had symptoms of or been treated for before obtaining your health insurance policy.
  • Daycare Procedures: Treatments or operations requiring admission to the hospital for less than 24 hours.
  • Pre-Hospitalization Expenses: Medical costs incurred before hospitalization, such as diagnostic tests.
  • Cumulative Bonus: Increase in sum insured by the insurer when no claims are made during the year, without additional premium charges.
  • Deductible: Amount paid out of pocket before the insurer covers expenses, typically chosen at policy purchase.
  • Sum Insured: Maximum coverage amount provided by the health insurer within a year.
  • Portability: Switching health insurers without losing out on waiting periods, enabling seamless transition.
